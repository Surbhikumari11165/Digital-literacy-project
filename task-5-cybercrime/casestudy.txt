UPI fraud is a rapidly increasing cybercrime in India due to the widespread use of digital payment systems. Fraudsters take advantage of people’s lack of awareness and trust in digital platforms.

In a typical case, attackers contact victims by pretending to be bank officials, customer support agents, or delivery personnel. They try to gain trust and then send a payment request or ask the user to scan a QR code.

Many users do not realize that entering a UPI PIN always authorizes a payment, not a receipt. As a result, victims unknowingly transfer money to the fraudster’s account.

In some cases, attackers also use phishing links or fake applications to steal login credentials and personal information. This makes the fraud more dangerous and difficult to detect.

This type of cybercrime mainly targets students, job seekers, and elderly individuals. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and risk of identity theft.

Therefore, it is important to stay alert and verify all transactions carefully before taking any action on digital payment platforms.
